My Project

  • The network design for RGUKT RK Valley utilizes a hybrid topology, blending star and mesh structures to maximize efficiency and reliability. Within each building, a star topology connects devices to central switches, allowing straightforward management and fault isolation, so individual device issues don’t impact the whole network.
    •  
  • For inter-building connections, a mesh topology links core devices, providing multiple pathways to ensure redundancy and high availability.
    •  
  • This approach supports scalability, enabling the addition of new buildings and devices with minimal disruption. The hybrid topology effectively balances simplicity in local areas with robust connectivity across the campus, meeting the demands of a modern educational network.
    •  
  • Dynamic routing protocols, such as OSPF (Open Shortest Path First) and RIP (Routing Information Protocol), are essential for automatically determining the most efficient routes for data transmission across complex networks. OSPF is highly scalable, making it ideal for large enterprise networks with multiple routers and areas, while EIGRP is known for its faster convergence times, which helps maintain network stability and performance even during topology changes or failures.
    •  
  • The use of these protocols together results in an adaptive, reliable, and efficient network routing system that supports seamless data flow across various departments and buildings in a large network environment.

  • The system allows users to search for nearby donors and blood banks online in real time without any cost.
